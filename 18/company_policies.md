# TechNova Inc. — Global Corporate Policies and Employee Handbook

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2025 | **Version:** 4.2 | **Approved By:** Board of Directors

---

## 1. Introduction

Welcome to TechNova Inc. This comprehensive Employee Handbook has been prepared by the Human Resources Department to provide all staff members with a clear and detailed understanding of the company's policies, procedures, benefits, and expectations. Every employee is required to read and acknowledge the contents of this document. The policies herein apply equally to all full-time, part-time, and contract employees across all global offices. Questions regarding interpretation should be directed to your regional HR Business Partner.

TechNova Inc. is committed to fostering an inclusive, productive, and transparent work environment. Our policies reflect this commitment, balancing organizational needs with employee well-being. This handbook supersedes all previous versions and will be reviewed annually by the Policy Review Committee. Any amendments will be communicated through the official HR Communications channel.

## 2. Leave and Attendance Policies

### 2.1 Annual Leave

All full-time employees are entitled to **24 days** of paid annual leave per calendar year, accrued proportionally at a rate of two days per month of active service. Annual leave does not include standard public holidays recognized by the company. Leave requests must be submitted through the HR portal at least five business days in advance for approval by the direct manager.

### 2.2 Sick Leave

Employees who are absent due to illness must notify their manager before the start of their scheduled shift. If an employee is absent for more than **3 consecutive sick days**, they are required to provide a formal medical certificate from a registered healthcare professional upon their return to work. The company provides up to 15 days of paid sick leave annually, separate from annual leave.

### 2.3 Extended Leave Approval

Any request for annual leave exceeding **7 consecutive leave days** requires explicit written approval from both the direct manager and the department head. Such requests must be submitted at least one calendar month in advance to allow for coverage planning and workload redistribution.

### 2.4 Leave Carryover

Employees are encouraged to utilize their full leave balance within the calendar year. However, a maximum of **5 unused annual leave days** may be carried forward into the first quarter (January through March) of the subsequent calendar year. Any carried-over days not used by March 31 will be forfeited without compensation.

### 2.5 Public Holidays

The company officially observes **11 standard public holidays** per calendar year across all offices. When a public holiday falls on a Saturday, it is observed on the preceding Friday; when it falls on a Sunday, it is observed on the following Monday. The list of recognized holidays is published on the intranet by November 1 each year.

### 2.6 Bereavement Leave

In the event of the death of an immediate family member (spouse, parent, child, or sibling), the company provides **5 days** of paid bereavement leave. For extended family members (grandparents, in-laws, aunts, uncles), two days of paid leave are available.

### 2.7 Unexcused Absences

Employees who fail to report to work and do not notify their supervisor for **3 consecutive unexcused working-day absences** will be considered to have voluntarily abandoned their position. Termination proceedings will be initiated automatically without further notice.

## 3. Working Hours and Remote Work

### 3.1 Standard Working Hours

The standard work schedule requires all employees to fulfill **40 working hours per week**, generally distributed from Monday through Friday. The standard office hours are 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM, which includes a mandatory one-hour lunch break that is not counted as working time.

### 3.2 Core Hours

While the company supports flexible scheduling, all employees must be available for meetings and collaborative work during **core hours starting at 10:00** (10 AM) and ending at 3:00 PM in their local time zone. Meetings should not be scheduled outside core hours without prior agreement from all participants.

### 3.3 Hybrid Work Policy

Under the current hybrid work framework, employees may work remotely for up to **2 days per week**. The remaining days must be spent at the designated corporate office. Remote work days must be agreed upon with the team manager and should not conflict with mandatory in-office collaboration days (typically Tuesday and Thursday).

### 3.4 Remote Work Stipend

To offset costs associated with working from home, all eligible employees receive a monthly internet and utilities stipend of **50 US dollars**, which is automatically added to their regular payroll deposit and is subject to applicable taxes.

## 4. Compensation and Benefits

### 4.1 Retirement Plan

TechNova Inc. offers a generous 401(k) retirement savings plan. The company matches employee contributions dollar-for-dollar up to **4 percent** of the employee's gross base salary. Employees are automatically enrolled upon completing their first month of employment and may adjust their contribution rate at any time.

### 4.2 Catch-Up Contributions

Employees who have reached the age of **50 years** become eligible for the catch-up retirement contribution program. Under this program, the company will match an additional two percent of catch-up contributions, above and beyond the standard four percent match.

### 4.3 Professional Development Budget

To foster continuous learning and career growth, each full-time employee is allocated an annual professional development budget of **2,000 US dollars**. This fund can be used for attending industry conferences, purchasing online course subscriptions, obtaining professional certifications, or buying technical books directly related to the employee's role.

### 4.4 Wellness and Fitness Subsidy

Physical and mental well-being are priorities at TechNova. Every employee is entitled to an annual wellness and fitness subsidy of **600 US dollars**, which may be applied to gym memberships, yoga classes, meditation apps, recreational sports leagues, or approved fitness equipment.

### 4.5 Commuter Transit Benefit

The company provides a pre-tax commuter transit benefit with a monthly limit of **130 US dollars** that covers public transportation costs including bus, subway, and regional rail passes. Employees must enroll through the benefits portal to activate this benefit.

### 4.6 Cost-of-Living Adjustment

Independent of performance-based raises and promotions, all employees receive a guaranteed annual cost-of-living salary adjustment of **3 percent**, applied during the April payroll cycle, to account for inflation and rising living expenses.

### 4.7 Peer Recognition — Kudos Program

Through the "Kudos Program," employees may nominate colleagues for outstanding teamwork, innovation, or customer service. Each month, selected winners receive a reward voucher valued at **100 US dollars**, redeemable at a range of partner retailers and online stores.

## 5. Family and Parental Support

### 5.1 Maternity Leave

Primary caregivers are eligible for **18 weeks** of fully paid maternity leave following the birth or legal adoption of a child. Leave may begin up to two weeks before the expected due date. Extensions may be requested as unpaid leave for an additional four weeks.

### 5.2 Paternity Leave

Non-primary caregivers are entitled to **14 days** of paid paternity leave, which may be taken at any time within the first six months following the birth or adoption of the child. This leave can be taken consecutively or split into blocks of no fewer than five consecutive days.

### 5.3 Parental Leave Notice

To ensure adequate coverage and smooth project handovers, employees must formally submit their parental leave request at least **60 days** in advance of the anticipated start date through the HR portal.

### 5.4 Emergency Childcare

Understanding the challenges facing working parents, TechNova partners with a national care provider network to offer up to **10 subsidized emergency childcare backup days** per family per calendar year. These can be used for unexpected school closures, caregiver illness, or similar emergencies.

## 6. Probation, Transfers, and Separations

### 6.1 Probation Period

All newly hired employees are subject to a probation period of **6 months** from their date of joining. During probation, performance reviews are conducted monthly. Either party may terminate the employment relationship with a notice period of one week during the probation period.

### 6.2 Resignation Notice Period

Employees wishing to resign must provide a minimum notice period of **30 days**. For Director-level positions and above, the notice period extends to 90 calendar days. During the notice period, the employee is expected to complete knowledge transfer and handover documentation.

### 6.3 Internal Transfers

Internal mobility is encouraged. However, employees must complete a minimum of **12 months** in their current role before becoming eligible to apply for internal job postings. This cooling-off period ensures adequate contribution to the current team.

### 6.4 Sabbatical Leave

Employees who have completed **60 months** (five years) of continuous service may apply for an unpaid sabbatical of up to three months for personal enrichment, travel, academic research, or community service. Sabbatical requests require VP-level approval.

### 6.5 Severance Policy

In the event of an involuntary separation due to restructuring or role redundancy, impacted employees will receive **2 weeks** of severance pay per full year of continuous service, plus continuation of health benefits for the equivalent period.

## 7. Performance and Discipline

### 7.1 Annual Performance Reviews

Formal performance evaluations are conducted once per year during December. Employees who receive an "Exceptional" performance rating are awarded a bonus multiplier of **15 percent** applied to their base annual bonus payout. Ratings of "Meets Expectations" receive the standard bonus without a multiplier.

### 7.2 Progressive Discipline

Unless the offense constitutes gross misconduct (such as theft, harassment, or data breach), the company follows a progressive disciplinary procedure consisting of exactly **3 formal warning strikes**: (1) a verbal warning documented in writing, (2) a written warning with a performance improvement plan, and (3) a final written warning. Failure to correct behavior after the third strike results in termination.

### 7.3 Crystal Obelisk Celebration

Dedication to the firm is highly valued. Upon successfully completing **15 years** of continuous service, an employee is celebrated at the annual gala and receives the prestigious Crystal Obelisk milestone award along with an additional equity grant.

## 8. Travel, Expenses, and Equipment

### 8.1 Business Dinner Reimbursement

When entertaining clients or attending approved business dinners, the company will reimburse meal expenses up to a maximum of **75 US dollars** per person for a single dinner. Receipts must be submitted along with the name of each attendee and the business purpose.

### 8.2 Domestic Travel Meals

For domestic business travel, the company provides a structured meal allowance (per diem) of **65 US dollars** per day to cover breakfast, lunch, dinner, and incidental food expenses. No receipts are required for per diem claims.

### 8.3 Expense Report Deadline

All out-of-pocket expenses and corporate credit card receipts must be submitted as a complete expense report within **14 calendar days** after returning from the business trip. Late submissions may result in delays or denial of reimbursement.

### 8.4 Home Office Equipment

New remote-eligible employees are entitled to a one-time home office equipment reimbursement of up to **500 US dollars**, which can be used to purchase an ergonomic chair, standing desk, external monitor, keyboard, or other approved peripherals.

### 8.5 Laptop Refresh Cycle

Engineering department employees receive a new company-issued laptop on a refresh cycle of every **3 years**. Non-engineering departments follow a four-year refresh cycle. All returned equipment must be wiped and submitted to the IT Asset Management team.

### 8.6 Relocation Support

Employees who are mandatorily transferred to a different corporate office will receive **4 paid moving days** to facilitate packing, travel, and settling into their new location, in addition to relocation expense reimbursement per the Relocation Policy addendum.

## 9. IT, Security, and Compliance

### 9.1 Cybersecurity Training

Every employee, regardless of department or seniority, is required to complete **2 hours** of mandatory cybersecurity awareness training each calendar year. This training must be completed during Q1 (January through March) and covers topics including phishing prevention, password hygiene, and data handling procedures.

### 9.2 Insider Trading Blackout

To comply with SEC regulations and prevent insider trading, all employees must observe a quiet period and are prohibited from trading company stock for **5 trading days** following the official release of quarterly earnings results.

### 9.3 IT Support SLA

The IT Help Desk operates under defined Service Level Agreements. Severity 1 (critical outage) tickets are addressed within one hour. Severity 2 (major issue) tickets are resolved within 24 hours. Severity 3 (low-priority) support tickets must be resolved within **7 calendar days**.

### 9.4 Disaster Recovery

TechNova's infrastructure is designed for high availability. If core production services experience **4 consecutive hours** of unplanned downtime, the automated disaster recovery protocol is triggered, failover to the secondary data center is initiated, and the Incident Commander is automatically paged.

### 9.5 Meeting Duration Policy

To combat meeting fatigue and protect deep work time, the corporate meeting policy states that no internal recurring meeting may exceed **90 minutes** of continuous duration without a mandatory ten-minute break. One-off workshops are exempt from this rule.

## 10. Onboarding and Miscellaneous

### 10.1 Benefits Enrollment Deadline

New hires have exactly **30 calendar days** from their official start date to log into the Benefits Portal and complete their mandatory benefits enrollment, including health insurance, dental coverage, vision plan, and retirement plan selections. Failure to enroll within this window results in default plan assignment.

### 10.2 Vision Care

Under the comprehensive vision care benefit, each employee may claim up to **250 US dollars** per calendar year for the purchase of prescription eyewear, including frames, lenses, and occupational computer glasses. Contact lenses are also covered.

### 10.3 Employee Hardware Discount

All full-time employees are entitled to a **25 percent** discount when purchasing TechNova-manufactured hardware products for personal use. This discount applies to a maximum of two devices per employee per calendar year and cannot be combined with other promotions.

### 10.4 Referral Bonus

Employee referrals are our most effective talent pipeline. A successful referral for a senior engineering position (who completes their full probation period) yields a referral bonus of **3,000 US dollars**, paid in the payroll cycle following confirmation.

### 10.5 Volunteer Time Off

TechNova strongly encourages community involvement. Each full-time employee is granted **16 hours** of paid volunteer time off per calendar year to participate in approved charitable, educational, or environmental service activities.

### 10.6 Overtime Eligibility

For hourly and shift-based employees, working **6 consecutive shifts** within a single workweek qualifies the employee for the overtime multiplier rate (1.5x) on the sixth shift and any subsequent shifts within that same week.

### 10.7 Tuition Reimbursement

The company reimburses **80 percent** of tuition costs for accredited university degree programs that are directly relevant to the employee's current role or anticipated career path within TechNova. Applications must be submitted before the start of the academic term.

### 10.8 Team-Building Events

Officially funded offsite team-building activities (such as escape rooms, cooking classes, or outdoor retreats) require a minimum team size of **8 employees** to qualify for the departmental entertainment budget allocation.
